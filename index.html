On the issues

Political Reform and Restoring Our Democracy

Its no secret that Illinois has experienced demoralizing political corruption for generations. We need to reform the system which keeps producing the same negative results and begin to restore our democracy. We simply cannot afford to wait for this change as millions of Illinois families continue to suffer due to longstanding political stagnation. There are many reforms that can be implemented right away to ameliorate this political blight.


Campaign Finance Reform

Perhaps the number one issue facing state and national politics is a broken campaign finance system that prioritizes money over people. In recognition of this disturbing reality, my campaign has committed to taking no corporate or special interest money. We need to restore integrity in government and bring regular people into the political process again. We must make YOUR voice matter, not just that of Springfield insiders and special interests.

This alone is not enough- we must fundamentally change the political system. Even given the Supreme Court’s ruling in the Citizens United case, we can still effect major change at the state level through small-donor funded elections. New York City’s Partial-Public Matching Program is a reform model that Illinois should embrace. Such a program would reduce conflicts of interest and return power to where it belongs: with the people, and not moneyed interests.


Ranked Choice Voting

Ranked Choice Voting, which was recently approved by voters in Maine, has the potential to transform politics in many positive ways. RCV has been shown to increase minority representation, make elections more civil, and allow third parties a greater chance to compete. Most of all, it would give you more choices, allowing you to select the candidate who best matches your views without "wasting" your vote or helping your least favorite candidate.


Ending Gerrymandering

We need to end gerrymandering, which creates unequal and unfair representation at all levels of government. Sadly, Illinois is a particularly notorious offender when it comes to drawing our districts along political lines. Our districts should be drawn by an independent commission so that voters pick their lawmakers, not the other way around.

Some have suggested that Illinois should not end its practice of gerrymandering, which helps Democrats in state, because Republicans in other states do the same thing. However, there is a potential solution to this problem in the form of interstate compacts against unfair districting. Illinois can lead by example, joining with other states, and starting the process of ending this heinous practice.


Other Measures to Increase Political Participation

We should do everything we can to ensure that all citizens can participate in the political process as both voters and candidates. Potential reforms to achieve this goal include making election day a statewide holiday, automatic voter registration, simplifying the state’s arcane ballot access rules and moving the date of primary elections from March to June, in order to allow candidates to share their message during warmer weather months.

Education

My education platform is based on the idea that Illinois should have the best school system in the nation. Every day, I see the amazing potential and promise of Chicago students, and it makes me both furious and heartbroken to see their futures compromised by political bickering. As a teacher in Chicago, I have firsthand knowledge of the challenges and potential solutions to improve our schools.


Fair Funding for Chicago’s Schools

Chicago students and taxpayers alike are victimized by the state’s current funding formula. Chicago currently serves about 20% of the state’s students, but receives only 15% of total state education dollars. It is unconscionable that our state representatives have allowed this to happen. We need to fundamentally reform the state’s school funding formula to ensure Chicago receives equitable funding including state support for the Chicago Teachers Pension Fund.


Expanding Early Childhood Education

Early childhood education is widely known to improve students’ emotional development and future academic success. Every family in Illinois should have access to high quality early childhood education. The state should increase funding for Pre-K education to reduce class sizes and attract high quality educators to nurture our youngest minds. This is one investment that is an easy call, the benefits of which will be fully realized as students transition into the K-12 public school system.


Social and Emotional Support for Our Students

Many Chicago students face unimaginable hardships at home but still show up to school every day. They are perhaps my greatest inspiration as a teacher. We need to ensure that every student has access to high quality social and emotional support. Access to counselors and social workers improves students’ emotional well being as well as academic performance. Every school should possess the financial means to hire a full time counselor and social worker.


Recruiting the Best Teachers Possible

A good teacher can truly transform lives. Chicago has an opportunity to lead the nation in cultivating and compensating teachers. Tuition for education preparation programs for future teachers should be completely tax deductible to ensure becoming a well-trained teacher is affordable.


Strengthening Neighborhood Schools

Our district is home to some of the finest elementary schools in the state. These schools need to be able to function without a fear of crippling funding shortages and yearly labor disputes. Our schools need dedicated funding streams without interference from local or state politicians. I support an elected school board for Chicago to make certain that educational experts and voters dictate school policy, not politicians.


Charter Schools

Charter schools can play a positive role in our education system by innovating and pioneering new models of learning and teaching. Many charters do this and serve low-income students admirably. That said, charters should not serve to exert downward pressure on teacher wages and benefits.

Given that they possess greater flexibility than traditional schools, charters should be held to higher proficiency and growth standards than other public schools. In addition, if a locality deems it appropriate to shut down a failing charter school, it should have wide leeway to do so without interference from the state.

Fixing the Budget and Making the Economy Work for Everyone

Illinois’s ongoing budget crisis is an embarrassment to our citizens, and current members of our legislature should be ashamed. Even worse, many of the most vulnerable members of society, including our children, have been needlessly harmed by political grandstanding. I support the following reforms to fix this situation as quickly as possible.


Creating a Fair Tax Code

According to the nonpartisan Institute for Taxation and Economic Policy, Illinois has the 5th most regressive taxation system in the country. This is primarily due to our high sales tax and low, flat rate income tax. I will fight for the creation of a progressive income tax to restore fairness to our tax code. This change will ensure that the wealthy pay their fair share, and allow us to raise much needed revenue to balance our budget without hurting lower income individuals.

Illinois also needs to close myriad corporate tax loopholes, which cost the state upwards of $2 billion annually. Many of these loopholes, such as the continental shelf exemption, provide no demonstrable economic benefit to the state, but exist as political gifts from dependent lawmakers. Eliminating these loopholes will save the state money and ensure that all businesses are treated fairly in Illinois.


Making investments for our future

There are numerous areas where we can make investments in our future and ensure that our economy works for all Illinois residents. Infrastructure improvements are vitally important to quality of life and public safety measures that would also help short-term employment in the state. I support expanding the Illinois Enterprise Zone Program, which provides regulatory relief and tax incentives to help revitalize communities with high levels of persistent poverty.


Raising the Minimum Wage

I ardently support the passage of a living wage law to ensure that every full time worker in our state can afford basic necessities. Such a law should be tailored to the price levels of the community in which a person lives so as to not adversely affect employment levels. In addition, the living wage should be indexed to inflation and automatically adjusted each year.


Pension Reform

Anyone who serves the public should be commended for their service and properly compensated. Unfortunately, our representatives made overly generous pension promises and failed to fully fund our pension funds for years, putting the state on the brink of insolvency. We can no longer continue to kick the can down the road. I support working with all stakeholders to find a permanent and sustainable solution to our pension crisis.
